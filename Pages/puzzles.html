The Pyramick (2020)

The pyramick is an original seven-axis puzzle that I first designed and 3D printed at home in mid-2020. It can be looked at as a pyraminx mech mod, or a hybrid between Uwe Meffert's Pyraminx and the "Gimmick" puzzle. Currently something big in the works with this puzzle! VIDEO

The Twisted Flame (2020)

The Twisted Flame is another puzzle that I first designed and 3D printed in mid-2020. It's another pyraminx modification-- in the same spirit as Eitan's twisted cube and twisted skewb, and QiYi's twisted skew, this is not quite a twisted-pyraminx-- more like a twisted windmill pyraminx. The Moldovian Pyramid (2020)

In 1982, after years of labor, Soviet toymaker Aleksandr Ordynets abandoned an almost-working prototype of his masterwork: the Moldovian Pyramid. In 2020, a bored American teenager used modern technology to design and build this version in two days, without the same fatal flaw.

Matchblokke (2017-19)

A puzzle I've revisited several times over the years, the 'Matchblokke' (could also be called a Split 1x3x4 or a Bandaged 2x3x4) is a deceptively complicated puzzle with a surprisingly simple mechanism. The first prototypes were designed piece-by-piece, but the most recent version uses more advanced design methodologies than brute force.

Helicoptrahedron (2018)

The helicoptrahedron is another shape transformation of a 12-axis puzzle into a tetrahedral shape, although unlike the reuleauxminx (see below), the twelve axes correspond to the faces of a rhombic dodecahedron, instead of a pentagonal dodecahedron-- introducing totally different pieces, face patterns, and move types.

The Reuleauxminx (2017)

Couldn't figure out whether I wanted to make a dodecahedral or tetrahedral puzzle, so this seemed like the obvious choice! It's a Face-Turning Dodecahedron transformed into a tetrahedron. Reuleauxminxes have been made before, but this is the first 3D printed one that I'm aware of. VIDEO
