Pinhole Cameras

Lensless cameras are easy to make, and can shoot quality photos on sheet or roll film. I built my first pinhole on a whim so that I could take old timey long exposure portraits of friends, only to discover the fascinating worlds of photography and camerabuilding. See my gallery, or my guide to building a pinhole film camera.

Modified Antique Cameras

Homemade pinhole cameras served as my gateway to lensed box cameras like the Kodak Brownie, the first mass-market camera. In 2023, I picked up a couple (one about seventy years old, the other 100) and modified them to take modern film. See my gallery, or my guide to modifying antique cameras for 35mm film.
