My name’s Ryan!

In 2015, I started using CAD to design and 3D Print Rubik’s Cube-type “Twisty Puzzles.”

Since then, I’ve 3D printed heat shields, fought the spread of fake news online, and tried to make microgravity drop towers obsolete-- winning awards at the California State Science Fair and Intel’s International Science & Engineering Fair (ISEF) along the way.

I’m currently working on what I call The Jurisdictional Clarity Project, a Python-based webtool that addresses the lack of district-specific data I encountered as an intern in the office of my state assemblymember.

I’m also lobbying for an amendment to California’s Constitution, ACA 4, which would allow 17-year-olds to participate in primary elections if they will be 18-years-old by the corresponding general election-- in 2018, I published op-eds making my case in The Sacramento Bee and the Los Angeles Times, and was later featured in The New York Times.
