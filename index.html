My name’s Ryan!

In 2015, I started using CAD to design and 3D Print Rubik’s Cube-type “Twisty Puzzles,” simultaneously founding an online Thingiverse subcommunity that today hosts over 1,300 puzzle-makers and 300+ original puzzle designs.

Since then, I’ve 3D printed heat shields, fought the spread of fake news online, and tried to make microgravity drop towers obsolete-- winning awards at the California State Science Fair and the International Science & Engineering Fair (ISEF) along the way.

I’m currently working on what I call The Jurisdictional Clarity Project, a Python-based webtool that addresses the lack of district-specific data I encountered as an intern in the office of my state assemblymember.

I’ve also lobbied for an amendment to California’s Constitution, ACA 4, which would allow 17-year-olds to participate in primary elections corresponding the general elections for which they will be 18-years-old-- in 2018, I published op-eds in The Sacramento Bee and the Los Angeles Times, and I was later interviewed for a New York Times piece on the issue.
